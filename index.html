FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I commission a piece?

Below you will find a formulary with all the data that is required to make a commission. You only need to fill every section and wait for the confirmation email. The confirmation email will include questions that may appear regarding your piece, changes if they're necessary and an estimation of the full price of the commission. After the price is settled, I'll send an invoice and you just need to pay and wait for updates that will be sent to your email. Basic commissions will only receive one 'work in progress' picture, while more complex commissions will require more communication between us to ensure everything is perfect.

I didn't receive the confirmation email, what should I do?

The process is not automated, I answer each email personally, so sometimes it will take a few days to get a confirmation. If your commission has an urgent deadline, don't worry, I answer the urgent commission emails first. If you don't receive a confirmation email after a week, contact me again with other email or through my social media. I will not answer questions related to commissions through social media, only problems related to the confirmation email.

How do I pay for a commmission?

All payments are made via Paypal only. After we have settled a price for the commission, I will send a Paypal invoice with the full price of the piece, including all extras needed.

How long will it take for my commission to be finished?

Depending on my workload and the complexity of your commission, it will take from one day to a few weeks to finish your piece. To ensure your commission is finished before a special date, you can add a deadline for an extra fee. If your commission hasn't started being done and you feel that it's taking too long, you're entitled to a full refund before I start working on it. I will periodically send screenshots of the piece while I'm working on it, so please be patient about it: if more than a week has passed without any emails regarding the status of your commission after I started working on it, you can send an email to ask for updates. Please refrain from sending several emails about the status of the piece.

Why did someone name appear on top of my spot in the waitlist?

Commissions are usually first come first serve, but sometimes there are urgent commissions (birthdays, holidays, weddings, etc.) that need to be done as soon as possible. Urgent commissions will be marked with an exclamation icon, and will appear in the upper spots of the list, and have an extra fee.

How do I cancel a commission?

You can simply send an email with your name and commission type and ask for a commission cancel.

How do refunds work?

If you have already paid half or the total price of the commission, and I've started working on your piece, we will need to discuss the percentage of the commission you will be refunded. This is done this way because of the ammount of work done: it's not the same to refund a piece that I'm still sketching than one that is halfway in the coloring phase. All refunds will be made through paypal I will not refund finished pieces after they have been finished, though you're entlited to make small changes to adjust the final piece.

Any attempt to try to get a full refund from Paypal of a piece that has been finished will be considered a scam.

If you're not happy about your commission or you have any problem with the final product, please contact me through my email and we will discuss it to reach a solution. Any suspicion of a scam will prevent you from making further commissions and will start legal action against the scammer.
